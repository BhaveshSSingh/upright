How it works

Three small things, working quietly.

Calibrate once. Upright handles the rest in the background — no dashboards, no streaks, no guilt.

1

It watches your eye level 🐾

Apple's on-device Vision framework tracks where your eyes sit against the line you calibrated. No video ever leaves your Mac.

2

A gentle nudge when you slouch

Drop below your line and a soft “Look up a bit!” appears. Straighten up and it quietly disappears — that's the whole interaction.

3

Camera off until it's needed

In Periodic mode the camera stays off and wakes for just a few seconds every few minutes — so that green light isn't staring at you all day.

⏱ 30 seconds, one time First launch

1Double-click Upright.pkg. macOS shows a scary popup — click Done. Not “Move to Trash”! Deep breaths. He's a good boy. 🐶
2Open System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down, click Open Anyway, and let the installer run.
3That's it — the installer un-quarantines the app, so Upright opens clean forever after. Launch it, allow camera, hit Calibrate.
🍺Terminal person? brew install --cask --no-quarantine <tap>/upright skips the popup entirely (tap coming soon).

🍎 about that popup Is this safe?

Apple charges $99/year to make that popup disappear. We did the math and chose dog treats instead. 🦴

The popup means “macOS doesn't know this developer” — not “this is malware.” Here's what Upright actually does:

  • ✓ 100% on-device. Your camera feed never leaves your Mac. Ever.
  • ✓ Zero network calls. No accounts, no analytics, no cloud. It couldn't phone home if it wanted to — it doesn't know your Wi-Fi exists.
  • ✓ Frames are thrown away instantly. Each one is checked for eye level, then gone. Nothing is recorded or stored.
